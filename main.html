Green Energy vs Clean Energy

Green energy and Clean energy are very similar in many ways. Both of them are common, Green energy is more targeted on producing energy using a certain method. Using a certain method, and pulling from a source helps to create energy that does not cause harm to nature. This natural energy is more clean and important, especially because of climate change. Even though these sources are not always renewable, they play a huge part in keeping our earth clean. Some examples of Green energy are solar and wind energy, geothermal energy, biomass systems, solar pannels, etc. Clean energy is also vitally important to our earth in many different ways. While Green focuses on keeping our environment clean, and less harmful, Clean specializes in keeping our air clean. This is important because to keep nature pure, we have to have oxygen so the plants and animals can thrive. Without oxygen we would not be able to make changes to better our earth. Some examples of clean energy are geothermal heat pumps, solar water heating, solar, and wind energy systems.

      

 

RESIDENTIAL SOLAR PANELS

These solar panels will absorb sunlight and convert it into electricity for the house. These solar panels will be installed on your roof.

SOLAR BATTERIES

Solar batteries main purpose is to store energy to be used in case of emergency or to be used at night. Excess energy can be sold to the main power grid.

MICRO WIND TURBINE GENERATORS

These wind turbine generators work the same way a regular wind turbine would work. Can be installed either on your roof or standing in your yard.

AIR SOURCE HEAT PUMP

Air source heat pumps will pull air directly from outside and convert that into heat wether it be heating the home or water. These units will be located outside of the home where there is good air flow.

      

GEOTHERMAL HEAT PUMP

Geothermal heat pumps pull air from 10-feet underground and converts this into heat. It’s said that this is a better option due to the ground having a more stable air temperature.

      

SMALL SCALE BIOMASS SYSTEMS

Biomass systems are taking organic materials, such as wood or plants, and creating energy out of it. Burning wood would be an example of this.

      

SOLAR HOT WATER HEATER

Energy from the sun will be used to heat water rather than using natural gasses or electricity. These systems could be mounted on your roof, side of house, or free standing in your yard.

      

RESIDENTIAL HYDROPOWER

This system will only be possible if you have some sort of water flow on your property. You could collect kinetic energy from these bodies of water and use it to power your home.
